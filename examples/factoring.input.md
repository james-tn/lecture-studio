# Source material: Difference of Squares

Audience: Algebra 1 students who already know how to multiply binomials.

Concept to teach: Factoring a difference of squares, using x^2 - 9 as the running example.

Key points the lecture should cover:
- A "difference of squares" has the form a^2 - b^2.
- In x^2 - 9, the first term is x^2 and 9 is a perfect square equal to 3^2.
- The factoring rule: a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b).
- Apply it: x^2 - 9 = (x - 3)(x + 3).
- Sanity check by expanding (x - 3)(x + 3): the -3x and +3x cancel, leaving x^2 - 9.
- Quick takeaway: whenever you see something squared minus something squared, it factors.

Tone: warm, encouraging, unhurried — like a favorite teacher at a whiteboard.
