# Source material: Deriving the Quadratic Formula

Audience: Algebra 1 / Algebra 2 students who can complete the square.

Goal: Derive the quadratic formula by completing the square on the general equation
a x^2 + b x + c = 0. This is a LONG derivation with many steps, so it should span
more than one whiteboard page. Start a fresh page at each major stage and keep the
current working equation pinned when you turn the page.

Stage 1 — Setup:
- Start from a x^2 + b x + c = 0 (with a not equal to 0).
- Divide every term by a:  x^2 + (b/a) x + (c/a) = 0.
- Move the constant to the right:  x^2 + (b/a) x = -(c/a).

Stage 2 — Complete the square:
- Take half of b/a, which is b/(2a), and square it to get b^2/(4a^2).
- Add b^2/(4a^2) to both sides.
- The left side becomes a perfect square:  (x + b/(2a))^2.
- The right side becomes  b^2/(4a^2) - c/a.

Stage 3 — Combine and solve:
- Put the right side over a common denominator:  (b^2 - 4ac)/(4a^2).
- So (x + b/(2a))^2 = (b^2 - 4ac)/(4a^2).
- Take the square root of both sides:  x + b/(2a) = ± sqrt(b^2 - 4ac) / (2a).
- Isolate x:  x = -b/(2a) ± sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)/(2a).
- Combine into the quadratic formula:  x = (-b ± sqrt(b^2 - 4ac)) / (2a).

Stage 4 — Recap:
- Box the final quadratic formula.
- Note that b^2 - 4ac is the discriminant, which tells how many real roots there are.

Tone: clear, encouraging, methodical — a confident teacher guiding a long derivation.
